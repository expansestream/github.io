Step 1

  • Turn on the power from the center control panel: Push top button first, middle button second.
  • Log into the left screen using the pin: 135013.
  • On the left screen, open Wirecast.
  • On the left screen, go to the top drop down menu File, and slide down to Open Recent.
  • Select the appropriate service document:
    • 10:30 service /Choral Eucharist.wcst
    • Wilderness /The Wilderness_backup.wcst
    • Weddings, funerals, special services /Choral Eucharist.wcst

Step 2

  • On the right screen, click on the tile overlay entitled Sermon.
  • A window entitled Shot Layers should appear.
  • Click on the heading entitled Preacher.
  • Click on the Layer Settings button (the 3 dots in the upper right-hand corner of the Shot Layers window).
  • A text box should appear.
  • Type in the name of the preacher delivering the sermon (found on the last page of the service leaflet) in the field Text:
  • Click on the Sermon overlay to close the Shot Layers window.

Step 3

  • On the right hand screen, move to the Title Overlays portion of the screen (containing opening screen, sermon, offertory, spiritual communion, closing screen, etc.).
  • Click the large plus sign (+).
  • A dialog box entitled Media Layer 1 should appear.
  • Select the option Media Files.
  • Select the option Browse Disk.
  • Open the Pictures folder.
  • Select the appropriate month.
  • Look at the various thumbnails. Each service has two thumbnails, one for Wirecast and one for Vimeo.
  • Select the appropriate Wirecast thumbnail: CE_[date] for the Choral Eucharist; WI_[date] for the Wilderness.

Step 4

  • Turn one's attention to the screen on the left.
  • Notice the box with all the buttons. This is the camera controller.
  • Turn it on by pressing the switch located in its back left corner.
  • The camera signals should load in Wirecast as the controller boots up.
  • Press the PMEM/TMEM located immediately to the right of the touch screen to load the camera presets.

Step 5

  • Again, direct attention to the screen on the left.
  • Open Google Chrome.
  • Click Vimeo in the Bookmarks bar.
  • A new Bookmarks bar entitled vimeo.comshould appear.
  • In the upper right-hand corner of the dialogue box, click the large blue button New Video.
  • In the drop-down menu, select Create Event.
  • In the resulting dialogue box, click Live Broadcast.
  • A new dialogue box will appear. Click the pencil icon at the top.
  • In the text box that appears, type the name of the service:
    • For the 10:30 service type Choral Eucharist, Rite II, [date of service]
    • For the Wilderness type The Wilderness, [date of service]
    • For other services type Name of Service, [date of service]
  • Click Save.

Step 6

  • Continue to direct attention to the screen on the left.
  • Click the button labeled Choose thumbnail.
  • Open the Pictures folder.
  • Select the appropriate month.
  • As in Wirecast previously, select the thumbnail for today's service and date (found in the appropriate month sub-folder):
    • T_CE_[date]
    • T_WI_[date]
  • Save, and the preview should change with the updated thumbnail.
  • While still in the dialogue box, make sure that Viewing privacy is set to Public.

Step 7

  • While still in the dialogue box, click the Destinations tab to connect to Facebook and YouTube.
  • Select the Saint John's Cathedral account for both and switch both on with the toggle button.

Step 8

  • Returning to the same dialogue box, select the Event tab.
  • A new URL link will be provided.
  • Copy this URL.
  • Open a new browser tab.
  • Paste the copied URL in the Bookmark bar and open it.
  • Press the D key on the keyboard.
  • In the small box appearing in the upper left-hand corner, copy the numeric Clip ID.
  • Keep this tab open in case one needs to copy the Clip ID again.

Step 9

  • Returning to the Bookmark tab entitled vimeo, click on the blue button Manage production.
  • Select Stream with encoder.

Step 10

  • Open a new browser tab, and click on the SJC WP Admin bookmark.
  • Login to the SJCathedral.org page using the pin: 135013.
  • In the upper left corner of the new dialogue box under Saint John's Cathedral, select Visit Site.
  • In the ensuing dialogue box, in the top menu bar, click Edit Page.

Step 11

  • In the ensuing dialogue box, scroll down to the section labeled Intro Settings, and select.
  • In the box labeled Video URL, highlight the string of numbers at the end and paste the Clip ID copied previously.
  • Click the blue Update button Update in the Publish dialogue box.
  • One can return to the homepage using the Visit Site button to assure that the homepage has been updated.

Step 12

  • About 10 minutes before the service begins, direct your attention to the screen on the right.
  • Click the Record button at the top.
  • In the emerging Output Settings window, in the Destination dropdown, select Record to Disk-MOV.
  • In the File field, click Browse, and select D:/ drive.
  • Click Save.
  • Click OK.
  • Ignore the warning message.
  • Click the Record button at the top again to begin recording.

Step 13

  • Continue with the screen on the right.
  • Click the Stream button at the top.
  • In the window that appears, go to the Destination dropdown and select RTMP server.
  • Leave this window open.

Step 14

  • Direct attention to the screen on the left.
  • Returning to the Bookmark tab entitled vimeo, click on the blue button Manage production.
  • Select Stream with encoder.
  • Copy the link labeled RTMPS URL and paste it into the Address section in the Output Settings window in Wirecast (the opened window on the right screen).
  • Copy the Stream Key from Vimeo and paste it into the Stream field in Wirecast (right hand screen).
  • Click OK.
  • Click Stream again at the top.
  • The Stream button will flash green before coming solid.
  • The stream is now live!

Step 15

  • Set output to Smooth in the Wirecast window (right-hand screen).
  • Open YouTube or Facebook on your mobile device to verify that the video is being broadcast.

Step 16

  • Once the service is over, end the broadcast by clicking the Stream and Record buttons in Wirecast (top of the right-hand screen).
  • Close the Wirecast window by clicking X in the upper right-hand corner.
  • Click Save or Don't Save (generally click Don't Save if new additions like thumbnails don't need to be saved).
  • Close Wirecast. Vimeo will automatically end the broadcast.
  • Close Google Chrome, and shut down the computer:
    • Turn off the power from the center control panel:
      • Push the middle button first.
      • Push the top button second.
  • You're done!

Powering Up - Powering Down

  • On the central console:
    • Press the top power button.
    • Press the lower power button.
  • On the left console:
    • Press the power button on the Wirecast Gear rack.

No audio on livestream

  • Confirm with sound engineer that the Stream volume is on.
  • In Wirecast, go to Layer 5 and click the microphone icon .
  • Green bars should appear in the Stream Preview window, going up and down.
  • Switch the preview to Live Video.

Cameras Not Loading

  • Close and re-open Wirecast.
  • Turn the camera controller off and then on.
  • Press the System button on the camera controller.
    • Turn the F1 knob until you see ALL OFF.
    • Push the knob in.
    • All cameras will power down.
    • Turn the F1 knob until you see ALL ON.
    • Push the knob in.
  • Cameras will reboot. It may take a few moments before the cameras load in Wirecast.

Problems Copying Clip-ID

  • Double click on string of numbers.
  • Copy everything that is highlighted.
  • Press the Microsoft key on the keyboard and then the R (run) key.
  • Type notepad into the dialog box.
  • Click OK.
  • Once the notepad app opens, paste the previously copied items.
  • In notepad, copy only the string of numbers.
  • Paste into Video URL described in Step 10.

Power to Dagwell Soundboard

  • Proceed to the back of the sound board.
  • Find the power cord.
  • Locate power switch above power cord.
    • Toggle switch to turn power on/off.

Arrival & Wirecast Setup

Step 1

  • If the computer is not on, boot it up.
  • Log in using the pin: 135013.
  • Open Wirecast.
  • Go to File > Open Recent.
  • Click the appropriate service document:
    • 10:30 service: /Choral Eucharist.wcst
    • Wilderness: /The Wilderness_backup.wcst
    • Dean's Forum: /Dean's Forum.wcst
    • Weddings, funerals, special services: /Choral Eucharist.wcst

Updating the Service Document

Step 2

  • Click on the title overlay in the first layer labeled Sermon.
  • Click Preacher.
  • Click on the Layer Settings button (the 3 dots in the upper right-hand corner).
  • In the Text field, update preacher as their name appears in the service leaflet.
  • Adjust text field if necessary.

Step 3

  • In the title overlay, click the large plus sign (+).
  • In the window that opens, go to Media Files > Browse Disk.
  • Open the Pictures folder (if not found, open the Downloads folder).
  • The thumbnails are arranged by month, and then by platform.
  • Look at the various thumbnails. Each service has two thumbnails, one for Wirecast and one for Vimeo.
  • Open the Wirecast folder.
  • Select the file for today's service:
  • Choral Eucharist: CE_[date]
  • Wilderness: WI_[date]
  • Dean's Forum: See thumbnails
  • Special Services: See thumbnails

Setting Up the Cameras

Step 4

  • Turn on the camera controller by pressing the switch located in its back left corner.
  • The camera signals will load in Wirecast once the controller boots up.
  • Press the PMEM/TMEM button to load the camera presets.

Connecting to Vimeo

Step 5

  • Open Google Chrome.
  • Click Vimeo in the Bookmarks bar.
  • Click the large blue button New Video.
  • Select Create Event.
  • On the next screen, click Live Broadcast.
  • Click the pencil icon at the top.
  • In the text box that appears, type the name of the service:
    • For the 10:30 service: Choral Eucharist, Rite II, [date of service]
    • For the Wilderness: The Wilderness, [date of service]
    • For the Dean's Forum: Dean's Forum with [Guest Name], [date]
    • For other services: Name of Service, [date of service]

Step 6

  • Beneath the video preview, click the button labeled Choose thumbnail.
  • Open the Pictures folder (if not found, open the Downloads folder).
  • As before, open the current month's folder, and select the appropriate thumbnail:
    • Choral Eucharist: T_CE_[date]
    • Wilderness: T_WI_[date]]
    • Dean's Forum: See correct thumbnail.
    • Special Services: See correct thumbnail.
  • Save, and the preview changes with the updated thumbnail.
  • Set Viewing privacy to Public.

Step 7

  • Click the Destinations tab.
  • Connect to Facebook and YouTube.
  • Select the Saint John's Cathedral account for both and switch both on with the toggle button.

Step 8

  • Select the Event tab.
  • Enter the Start date and Time of the stream at the bottom.
  • Copy the link provided.
  • Paste and open in a new tab.
  • Press the D key on the keyboard.
  • In the upper left-hand corner, copy the numeric Clip ID.
  • Keep this tab open in case the Clip ID needs to be copied again.

Updating the Saint John's Homepage

Step 9

  • Special Services have their own homepage and not the SJC homepage.
  • Open a new browser tab, and click on the SJC WP Admin bookmark.
  • Login to the SJCathedral.org page using the provided credentials.
  • In the upper left corner under Saint John's Cathedral, select Visit Site.
    • If streaming a wedding, type https://sjcathedral.org/wedding-service/ into the address bar, and enter.
    • If streaming a funeral, type https://sjcathedral.org/funeral-service/ into the address bar, and enter.
    • If streaming a special event, type https://sjcathedral.org/special-event/ into the address bar, and enter.
  • On the next page, in the top menu bar, click Edit Page.

Step 10

  • In the editing page that loads, scroll down to the section labeled Intro Settings.
  • In the box labeled Video URL, highlight the string of numbers at the end and paste the Clip ID copied previously.
  • Click the blue Update button Update to save changes.
  • Return to the homepage using the Visit Site button to assure that the homepage has been updated.

Starting the Stream

Step 11

  • About 10 minutes before the service begins, begin the stream by clicking the Record button.
  • In the Output Settings window, in the Destination dropdown, select Record to Disk-MOV.
  • In the File field, click Browse, and select D:/ drive.
  • Click Save.
  • Click OK.
  • Click the Record button again at the top to begin recording.

Step 12

  • Click the Stream button at the top.
  • In the window that appears, go to the Destination dropdown and select RTMP server.
  • Leave this window open.

Step 13

  • Go back to Vimeo, click on the blue button Manage production.
  • Select Stream with encoder.
  • Copy the link labeled RTMPS URL and paste it into the Address section in the Output Settings window in Wirecast.
  • Copy the Stream Key from Vimeo.
  • Go to the Destination dropdown.
  • Paste the stream key into the stream field in Wirecast.
  • Click OK.
  • Click Stream again at the top.
  • The Stream button will flash green before coming solid.
  • The stream is now live!

Step 14

  • Set output to Smooth in the Wirecast window.
  • Open YouTube or Facebook on your mobile device to verify that the video is being broadcast.

Streaming Dean's Forum Screen Presentation

Step 15

  • On the Dagwell computer, open Novocast.
  • Select Dagwell Projector.
    • This will project the presentation onto the screen in house.
    • The presentation is streamed via screen capture.
  • In Wirecast, click the Screen Capture function in Layer 2.
    • This will record whatever is showing on the main (left) monitor.
    • Be sure to have the presentation open on the left screen!
  • The option in Layer 3 is activated by clicking one of the two video options in Layer 2.

Ending the Stream

Step 16

  • Once the service is over, end the broadcast by clicking the Stream and Record buttons in Wirecast.
  • Close the Wirecast window by clicking X in the upper right-hand corner.
  • Click Save or Don't Save (generally click Don't Save if new additions like thumbnails don't need to be saved).
  • Close Wirecast. Vimeo will automatically end the broadcast.
  • While closing Vimeo, click End Event in the upper right-hand corner.
  • Close Google Chrome, and shut down the computer.
  • You're done!
